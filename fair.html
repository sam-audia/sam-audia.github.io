Abstract

With 3D printers becoming cheaper, they are finding their ways into more and more makerspaces and even homes. Though the price has become more accessible, there is still a steep learning curve in order to use a 3D printer effectively. Here is where the 3D Print a Week Challenge comes in. Over the course of six weeks, you create six unique prints after exploring a short lesson in one of three categories: software, techniques, or materials. In the past, design challenges have been used to quickly and efficiently give people experience in skill based practices, such as graphic and app design. The 3D Print a Week Challenge is unique in that it is using a design challenge to teach a topic more closely related to stem in hopes of expanding the knowledge base and accessibility of 3D printing. Ultimately, this challenge hopes to bring more people into 3D printing and the highly collaborative maker community.
